## 25 · Response — "Why not just use ChatGPT?"

Totally fair — generic AI is genuinely good at money *concepts*. The difference is grounding: VAULT's Concierge can see your actual balances and accounts (read-only), so "can I afford X?" gets answered with your numbers instead of a template — and when it doesn't have the data, it says so rather than guessing. The other half is that VAULT doesn't wait to be asked: the moves surface from your accounts each morning before you'd have thought to prompt anything. Use both, honestly — ChatGPT for concepts, VAULT for *your* situation.

## 26 · Response — "Why should I connect my bank?"

You don't have to — that's real: three questions get you a starting score in 60 seconds with no bank login, and the app is honest that it's an estimate. Connecting is what turns generic guidance into *your* moves: your idle cash with the actual amount, your utilization with the actual percentage, the account type you're actually missing. The connection is read-only through Plaid — look, never touch — your credentials never reach us, and disconnecting or deleting everything is one tap in Settings. Take the 60-second version first; connect if the app earns it.

## 27 · Response — "Is this financial advice?"

No — and the app says so explicitly, not in fine print. VAULT is educational: it shows you what's in your own data (an idle balance, a utilization percentage, an account type you don't have), explains the general principles, and leaves the decision and the action with you. It never manages money, never executes anything, and for major decisions — taxes, big investments, debt restructuring — it will point you toward a qualified professional, because that's what those decisions deserve.

## 28 · Response — "How is this different from Rocket Money or Monarch?"

Different job. Tracking apps are built to *show* you your money — budgets, categories, reports — and subscription apps are built to execute one specific chore for you. VAULT is built around what happens next: it reads your accounts and deals you specific moves each day — subscriptions are one card in the deck, not the product — and *you* act on them with exact instructions, because VAULT is read-only by design and never acts on your accounts. If you love deep budget tracking, a tracker may suit you (some people run both). If your problem is "I see all my money and still don't know what to do," that's the exact gap VAULT exists for.
